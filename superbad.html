Superbad

A stunning critique of policing culture in New England, America. Seth Rogan writes and acts in this comedy that is truly ahead of its time with its (unintentional) critique of policing behaviour and its impression on America youths (main youths are played by Jonah Hill, Michael Cera, and Christopher Mintz-Plasse). Emma Stone has an underrated performance, doing a great job represeting '80sish/'90ish/'00ish teen ladies with her scoffs and banged coiff. Watching it in 2021, my overwhelming feeling is that the writer's lagged in equality for all bodily fluids: periods and ejaculation should be equally meh or equally gross (For an example of such equality, see Big Mouth). For an interesting comparative of the North American highschooler experience of 1980s male versus the 2010s female, watch Superbad back to back with Booksmart.
