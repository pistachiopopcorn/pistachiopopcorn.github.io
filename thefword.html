The F Word (aka "What If?")

Zoey Kazan strikes again in a movie that clearly illustrates the not-entirely-honest place, deep down inside ourselves, where we can find our desire to be "just friends" with the opposite sex. It's filmed in a similar style to When Harry Met Sally (1989) - the audience watches the tragically long process of both protagonists coming to terms with their feelings for one another through conversations on long walks in the city and in backroom discussions with their bestfriends. The absurdism of Adam Driver and Mackenzie Davis (the bestfriends in question) provides comic relief to "the good friend/guy" played by Daniel Radcliffe and "the innocent/provocative girl" played by Kazan. The cinematography creatively depicts timelines and secret thoughts. And with its good acting and simple but strong storyline, this movie returns 21st century romantic comedy to a more classic time.
