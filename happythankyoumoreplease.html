Happy. Thank You. More Please.

A movie that highlights the modern, straight, cis-gendered, heterosexual, female experience living the big city. It explores the concept of "letting go" - of judgement, of preconceived notions of ourselves, of our expectations of relationships - and how powerful this can be in challenging the superficiality of our current world. Overall, it's like Josh Radner screenplay is based on snippets of eavesdropped conversations - on late night strolls and through cubicle walls (of the work and bathroom variety). Some of the dialogue isn't super relatable - the million dollar words may roll off Zoe Kazan's tongue but likely no one else's in real life. But look out for my favourite, simple line that Kazan utters: "Whisper it to me." I'd challenge you to find a more intimate yet pure moment in modern cinema.
